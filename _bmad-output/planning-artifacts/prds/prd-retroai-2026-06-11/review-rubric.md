# PRD Quality Review — retroai

## Overall verdict
This is a tight, well-grounded MVP PRD: the thesis (channel-by-role over a shared data model) is stated and threads through every feature, the Glossary is used with discipline across FRs/UJs/SMs, and assumptions/open questions are honestly tagged and indexed. The main risks are a few FRs whose "done" line still depends on unresolved Open Questions (FR-3, FR-6, FR-13), and one Out-of-Scope item (`[NOTE FOR PM]` on Bot-origin Turno editing) that looks like a real near-term tension dressed as a footnote. Decision-readiness is otherwise strong; this PRD is buildable with a short list of pre-architecture resolutions.

## Decision-readiness — adequate
The PRD largely avoids the "everything balances" trap. Trade-offs are named with real costs: §5 Non-Goals explicitly states "App móvil/web dedicada para el Jugador — decisión de diseño (no limitación temporal)" and §10 names a competitive timing risk ("actores establecidos... podrían replicar el ángulo de roster/bot si el lanzamiento se demora"). Open Questions in §8 are genuinely open (e.g., OQ1 on Suplentes ordering, OQ5 deferring the WhatsApp API approach to Architecture against a "no paid external services" constraint).

However, a few items are framed as resolved when they're load-bearing decisions still pending:

### Findings
- **medium** Bot-origin Turno editing deferred via `[NOTE FOR PM]` inside Out-of-Scope (§6.2) — "Edición/cancelación de Turnos de Origen Bot desde el Panel (`[NOTE FOR PM]`: si surge como necesidad temprana del piloto, revisar...)". This reads like a real operational gap (what happens when an Empleado needs to fix a Bot-origin Turno with a typo or a no-show that the Bot didn't catch?) parked as a "revisit if it comes up" rather than a decision with a stated fallback. *Fix:* Either commit to a fallback (e.g., "Empleado can mark Bot-origin Turno as Pagado/cancelled via Panel even though Roster stays read-only") or promote this to an Open Question with an owner and trigger condition.
- **low** FR-6's NFR ("Solo Dueño puede crear/editar Turnos Fijos") is itself flagged `[ASSUMPTION]` with a noted tension — "la creación de Turnos Fijos ocurre desde Calendario; ver §9" — but the PRD doesn't say what happens if an Empleado taps the "mark recurrent" action on a Turno they created. *Fix:* one sentence on whether the UI hides/disables the recurrence toggle for Empleado, or whether it's caught server-side only.

## Substance over theater — strong
No findings. Three personas (Capitán/Jugador, Administrador/Dueño, Empleado), each driving distinct FRs and UJs — none feel decorative. The Vision (§1) is specific to this product: it names the actual competitors with their actual complaints ("comisiones, cobros incorrectos y soporte deficiente"), the actual market data (2-3M players, +30% canchas), and the specific unmet need ("ninguna resuelve el roster de un grupo fijo recurrente") — this could not swap into another PRD unchanged. NFRs are scoped to specific features rather than boilerplate (e.g., §4.1 "Las notificaciones del Bot... deben llegar en segundos, no minutos").

## Strategic coherence — strong
The thesis — "diferenciación de canal por rol... sobre un modelo de datos y lógica de negocio compartidos" (§1) — is explicit and every feature traces back to it: Bot features (4.1) serve the Jugador/Capitán channel, Panel features (4.2-4.5) serve Administrador/Empleado, and §7's SM-1 directly measures channel adoption ("% de Turnos... con Origen Bot"), which is the thesis's central bet, not a vanity metric. Counter-metrics SM-C1/SM-C2 are not boilerplate — they specifically guard against the thesis being satisfied "on paper" while the underlying user pain (capitán's time burden, mostrador friction) persists. MVP scope kind reads as problem-solving (replace cuaderno + manual roster chasing), and the in/out-of-scope split (§6) matches that — pricing engines, multi-complex networking, and online payments are all cut as orthogonal to the core problem.

## Done-ness clarity — adequate
Most FRs have genuinely testable consequences (e.g., FR-1's "El Bot... reporta al Capitán cualquier entrada inválida o duplicada sin enviar nada hasta que se corrija" is verifiable; FR-9's "sin necesidad de recargar la pantalla" is a clear bound). However:

### Findings
- **high** FR-3 (Reemplazo auto-gestionado) has two consequences whose actual trigger conditions are `[ASSUMPTION]`-tagged and unresolved — "El cupo liberado se ofrece a los Suplentes en el orden/criterio definido (`[ASSUMPTION]`...)" and "Si ningún Suplente confirma dentro del plazo definido (`[ASSUMPTION]`...)". An engineer cannot build the replacement-offer logic or the timeout-to-"Sin cubrir" transition without these values. This is correctly flagged as Open Question 1, but it means FR-3 — one of the three FRs validating the primary success metric SM-1 — is not yet "done"-testable as written. *Fix:* resolve OQ1 before story creation for FR-3, even if the resolution is itself a configurable default (e.g., "Suplentes ordered as listed by Capitán; 2-hour timeout, configurable later").
- **medium** FR-6's "done" condition for editing recurring instances depends on an unresolved data-model question — "Administrador puede modificar o cancelar instancias futuras individualmente sin afectar el Turno recurrente original (`[ASSUMPTION]`, ver §9)" — correctly mirrored as Open Question 3, but until Architecture resolves it, the consequence as written can't be verified as "instance vs. series" semantics aren't pinned down.
- **low** FR-13's invitation-link consequence — "Un link/código de invitación usado o expirado no permite crear una cuenta nueva (`[ASSUMPTION]` sobre expiración...)" — is testable in shape but the actual expiration semantics (single-use vs. time-limited vs. both) are deferred to Architecture per §9. Low severity because the testable boundary (link becomes invalid after some condition) is stated even if the exact condition isn't.

## Scope honesty — strong
No findings beyond what's noted above. §5 Non-Goals is substantive (7 items, each with a one-line rationale, not just a list of feature names). The Assumptions Index (§9) has 10 entries, each tagged inline and cross-referenced to the relevant FR/section, several explicitly linked to Open Questions. Given this is a single-pilot, single-developer MVP with direct owner access (§10), the open-items density (5 OQs + 10 ASSUMPTIONs + 2 NOTE FOR PM-equivalents across the whole doc) is proportionate — high enough to show real unresolved tensions, not so high it reads as unfinished thinking.

## Downstream usability — strong
This PRD is chain-top (feeds UX → architecture → stories), and it's built for that. The Glossary (§3) is used with notable consistency — terms like **Turno**, **Roster**, **Origen**, **Estado de Confirmación**, **Estado de Pago**, **Suplente** appear capitalized and identically across UJs, FRs, and SMs throughout. FR IDs (FR-1 through FR-14) and UJ IDs (UJ-1 through UJ-7) are contiguous and each FR states which UJ(s) it "Realiza." Cross-references to `EXPERIENCE.md`/`DESIGN.md` mocks are specific (file names, flow numbers) rather than vague "see UX doc."

### Findings
- **low** UJ-4 through UJ-7 don't follow the same "Persona + contexto / Entry state / Path / Climax / Resolution / Edge case" structure as UJ-1–UJ-3 — they're condensed to one paragraph each. This is reasonable since §2.3 explains they "mirroran 1:1 los Key Flows de EXPERIENCE.md," but it does mean UJ-4–7 carry less standalone context if EXPERIENCE.md isn't at hand. Each does have a named protagonist (Marcela, Hernan), so the core requirement is met — this is a stylistic/depth note, not a structural gap.

## Shape fit — strong
The PRD correctly mixes shapes: UJs with named, recurring protagonists (Diego, Lucía, Nicolás for the Bot side; Marcela, Hernan for the Panel side) are load-bearing for a consumer-facing/multi-stakeholder product, matching the "Consumer product / multi-stakeholder B2B" guidance. At the same time, the Panel side leans toward capability-spec framing where appropriate (FR-11–FR-14 on auth/roles read as a capability list, which fits since they're infrastructure rather than user-journey-driven). Brownfield concerns don't apply (greenfield). The single-complejo, single-developer constraint (§10) is acknowledged honestly without inflating the PRD with multi-tenant ceremony it doesn't need yet — while the Guardrails section preserves the future multi-complejo path without building for it now ("El modelo de datos no debe acoplar la identidad del Jugador al Complejo único del MVP de forma irreversible"). No over- or under-formalization observed.
